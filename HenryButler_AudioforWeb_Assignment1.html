Audio Recordings of New York City Squares

Exploring the Spatial Character of Field Recordings

Description:

This project allows listeners to explore the soundscapes of three iconic New York City Squares (Tompkins Square, Union Square, and Washington Square).
Listeners can switch between Mono, Stereo, and Binaural recordings to see how the representation of sound in the space changes with each recording technique.
Each set of recordings is accompanied by a snapshot to give the lister a visual context to the sounds happening around them.
While the photo will provide some context, the audio will lead the way in telling the story.

Mono recording was captured with one dynamic microphone facing straight ahead (from perspective of the image).

Stereo recording was captured with two condenser microphone capsules in an XY configuration (left facing 45 degrees to left from straight ahead, right facing 45 degrees to right from straight ahead).

Binaural recordings were captured using a Sennheiser Ambeo headset with a omni direction microphone capsule placed in each ear. This is meant to record in the same way our ears naturally capture sound.

In order to get the full spatial effect, you must listen to the audio over headphones!
